Apapransa


Description

Apapransa, also known as Apransa or Oprapransa, is a traditional Ghanaian dish popular among the Akan people, especially in the Ashanti region.
This hearty meal combines roasted corn flour with palm nut soup, creating a thick, flavorful porridge-like consistency.
Apapransa is often enjoyed as a comfort food and is known for its rich, nutty flavor and satisfying texture.


Ingredients

  • 2 cups roasted corn flour (ground roasted corn)
  • 4 cups palm nut soup base
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon palm oil
  • 1 smoked fish or 1 cup smoked meat (optional)
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 teaspoon ground pepper (preferably Grains of Paradise)
  • 2 cups water

Steps

  1. In a large pot, heat the palm oil over medium heat.
  2. Add the chopped onion and minced garlic. Saut√© until the onion is translucent.
  3. Pour in the palm nut soup base and bring to a gentle boil.
  4. If using, add the smoked fish or meat to the boiling soup and let it simmer for about 10 minutes.
  5. Gradually add the roasted corn flour to the soup, stirring constantly to avoid lumps.
  6. Continue stirring as the mixture thickens. Add water as needed to achieve desired consistency.
  7. Season with salt and ground pepper to taste.
  8. Reduce heat to low and let the Apapransa simmer for about 15-20 minutes, stirring occasionally to prevent sticking.
  9. The final consistency should be thick and smooth, similar to a dense porridge.
  10. Remove from heat and let it cool slightly.
  11. Serve hot, often shaped into balls or mounds, alongside additional palm nut soup if desired.

*Note* Apapransa is typically eaten with the hands, often rolled into small balls and dipped in extra palm nut soup.
