Jollof Rice


Description

Jollof rice is a beloved West African dish, particularly popular in Nigeria, Ghana, and Senegal.
This flavorful one-pot meal features rice cooked in a rich tomato sauce, resulting in a vibrant orange-red color and a complex, savory taste.
Often served at celebrations and gatherings, jollof rice is a staple in West African cuisine and has gained international recognition for its delicious flavor profile.


Ingredients

  • 3 cups long-grain parboiled rice
  • 1 can (400g) crushed tomatoes
  • 1 red bell pepper, chopped
  • 2 medium onions, chopped
  • 3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 1/2 cup vegetable oil
  • 2 cups chicken or vegetable broth
  • 2 teaspoons curry powder
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 2 bay leaves
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 scotch bonnet pepper (optional, for heat)

Steps

  1. Blend the crushed tomatoes, red bell pepper, and one chopped onion until smooth.
  2. Heat the vegetable oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the remaining chopped onion and saut√© until translucent.
  3. Add the minced garlic and cook for another minute.
  4. Stir in the tomato paste and cook for 2-3 minutes until it darkens slightly.
  5. Pour in the blended tomato mixture and cook for about 10-15 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  6. Add the curry powder, thyme, bay leaves, salt, and pepper. Stir well.
  7. Wash the rice until the water runs clear, then add it to the pot. Stir to coat the rice with the sauce.
  8. Pour in the chicken or vegetable broth. If using, add the whole scotch bonnet pepper at this point.
  9. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce heat to low. Cover and simmer for about 25-30 minutes, or until the rice is cooked and the liquid is absorbed.
  10. Remove from heat and let it stand, covered, for 5-10 minutes.
  11. Fluff the rice with a fork, remove the bay leaves and scotch bonnet pepper (if used), and serve hot.~
