Waakye


Description

Waakye is a beloved Ghanaian dish that combines rice and beans, creating a flavorful and hearty meal. This popular street food is known for its distinctive reddish-brown color, achieved by cooking the rice with dried sorghum leaves. Waakye is typically served with a variety of accompaniments, making it a versatile and satisfying dish enjoyed throughout Ghana and beyond.

  • 2 cups long-grain rice
  • 1 cup black-eyed peas or red beans
  • 4-5 dried sorghum leaves (or 1 tbsp red sorghum powder)
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 4 cups water
  • 1 tbsp vegetable oil

Steps

  1. Soak the beans overnight or for at least 4 hours.
  2. Rinse the soaked beans and place them in a large pot with 2 cups of water.
  3. Add the dried sorghum leaves or powder to the pot.
  4. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for about 30 minutes or until beans are partially cooked.
  5. Add the rice, salt, remaining water, and vegetable oil to the pot.
  6. Stir gently and bring to a boil again.
  7. Reduce heat, cover the pot, and let it simmer for about 20 minutes or until rice is cooked and water is absorbed.
  8. Remove from heat and let it stand, covered, for 5 minutes.
  9. Fluff the waakye with a fork and serve with your choice of accompaniments like shito (hot pepper sauce), fried plantains, or boiled eggs.
